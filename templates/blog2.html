Pakistan Tax Rates for Individuals (Tax Year 2024): A Detailed Overview

Understanding tax obligations is vital for prudent financial management. Here's a comprehensive breakdown of tax rates for individual taxpayers in Pakistan for the tax year 2024, distinguishing between salaried and non-salaried earners.


Tax Slabs for Non-Salaried Taxpayers

Non-salaried taxpayers, such as business owners and freelancers, are subject to progressive tax slabs:

  • Up to PKR 600,000: No tax.
  • PKR 600,000 - 800,000: 7.5% of the exceeding amount.
  • PKR 800,000 - 1,200,000: PKR 15,000 plus 15% of the exceeding amount.
  • PKR 1,200,000 - 2,400,000: PKR 75,000 plus 20% of the exceeding amount.
  • PKR 2,400,000 - 3,000,000: PKR 315,000 plus 25% of the exceeding amount.
  • PKR 3,000,000 - 4,000,000: PKR 465,000 plus 30% of the exceeding amount.
  • Above PKR 4,000,000: PKR 765,000 plus 35% of the exceeding amount.

Tax Slabs for Salaried Taxpayerss

Salaried individuals enjoy relatively lower tax rates:

  • Up to PKR 600,000: No tax.
  • PKR 600,000 - 1,200,000: 2.5% of the exceeding amount.
  • PKR 1,200,000 - 2,400,000: PKR 15,000 plus 12.5% of the exceeding amount.
  • PKR 2,400,000 - 3,600,000: PKR 165,000 plus 22.5% of the exceeding amount.
  • PKR 3,600,000 - 6,000,000: PKR 435,000 plus 27.5% of the exceeding amount.
  • Above PKR 6,000,000: PKR 1,095,000 plus 35% of the exceeding amount.

Non-salaried individuals face a progressive tax structure, with rates starting at 0% and reaching 35%. Salaried taxpayers experience similar rates, but with the advantage of lower rates if their salary constitutes at least 75% of their total taxable income
